Chamber Music

Music is also a big part of my life

Outside of my academic pursuits, I spend most of my free time in front of the piano. I especially enjoy classical chamber music, which has long been a source of reflection and an outlet for creativity.

I have always valued chamber music above solo performance—playing with others brings a sense of dialogue, devotion, and shared happiness that no individual practice can replace.

I was never professionally trained, only received a brief 6-year formal instruction in my childhood. The boredom of the lessons has unfortunately delayed my rediscovery of the piano until high school.

During my undergraduate studies, I formed Paper Trio with close friends— violinist Yue Zhang and cellist Binyue Lu. Together, we explored works by Schubert, Brahms, Beethoven. This has been my best memory in college.

Those years of collective rehearsal and sight-reading were formative, not only in developing my musical sensitivity but also in shaping how I value collaboration. Currently, I am collaborating with local soprano Liudmila Arno, actively performing in Lausanne, Switzerland.
